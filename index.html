bio

What I love most is deciphering hard problems, especially the kind that only yield to a Sherlock Holmes style of investigation. Following clues, testing hypotheses, and uncovering hidden structure is the way I most naturally work, and it explains almost everything about my path. In high school, that instinct first found a home in physics, where I trained through olympiad competition, ranked in the national top ten in China, and received the highest experimental score. I later studied computer science, moved into electrical engineering, and during my Ph.D. gravitated toward uncertainty in complex systems and some of the early attempts to bring deep models into electrical engineering problems.

In industry, I started as an algorithm engineer, but I was quickly drawn to AI systems as a whole. To understand that stack properly, I taught myself high-performance computing, operator optimization, framework design, drivers, and hardware. Over time, I learned to contribute across the stack, and that remains the core of how I work today.

featured projects

flagship artifact

CroqTile: a next-gen programming language for AI kernels and accelerators

A GPU and accelerator programming language centered on TileFlow and DMA-oriented abstractions. It targets higher productivity without giving up low-level control or near-vendor performance.

  • About 5x productivity and around 40% of equivalent CUDA code.
  • Near-zero abstraction cost on published cases.
  • 353 compile-time checks and 1,319 runtime assertions.
  • AI-driven tuning results reported from 671 to 1127 TFLOPS in 68 iterations.
